# Product

## Register

product

## Users

AlFaris ("الفارس") is used by the factory's high-level management members. They work in an Arabic-first, right-to-left interface to review factory performance, manage operational records, monitor accounts and expenses, coordinate work, and make informed management decisions efficiently.

## Product Purpose

AlFaris is a simple factory management system that consolidates the factory's essential management workflows into one dependable application. Its dashboard, PrimeNG-based controls, data grids, reports, and custom interface styles should help management understand current operations quickly and complete routine administrative work with minimal friction.

Success means users can find important information, understand factory status, and complete management tasks without training, visual distraction, or unnecessary steps.

## Brand Personality

Clear, efficient, simple, and professional. The interface should communicate operational confidence through precise Arabic copy, familiar controls, strong information hierarchy, and restrained presentation.

## Anti-references

Avoid interfaces that visibly resemble generic AI-generated designs. Do not use decorative glow effects, excessive gradients, glassmorphism, ornamental dashboard decoration, inflated card layouts, or visual novelty that competes with operational data. Avoid complexity disguised as sophistication and interactions that require users to relearn standard management-software conventions.

## Design Principles

1. Put decisions before decoration: emphasize the information and actions management needs now.
2. Make common work obvious: use familiar patterns, direct Arabic labels, and predictable interaction behavior.
3. Keep complexity contained: advanced grids, reports, and charts should remain understandable at a glance.
4. Preserve professional restraint: use visual emphasis deliberately and avoid effects that do not communicate state or meaning.
5. Design Arabic-first: treat RTL layout, Arabic typography, and local reading patterns as foundational rather than adaptations.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Support complete keyboard navigation, visible focus states, semantic structure, sufficient color contrast, accessible form labels and validation, screen-reader-compatible controls, and reduced-motion preferences. Do not rely on color alone to communicate status. Maintain correct Arabic RTL reading and navigation order across desktop and responsive layouts.
